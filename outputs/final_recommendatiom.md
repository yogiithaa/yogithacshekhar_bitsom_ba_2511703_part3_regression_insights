# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

The objective of this analysis was to identify factors associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores.

Regression analysis indicates that footfall, marketing spend, and inventory availability are the most important variables associated with sales performance.

---

## Factors Most Strongly Associated With Monthly Sales

### Footfall

Footfall emerged as the strongest predictor of sales.

Stores attracting more customers generally achieve higher sales volumes.

### Marketing Spend

Marketing expenditure shows a positive association with sales performance.

Increased marketing investment appears to support customer acquisition and revenue generation.

### Inventory Availability

Stores with higher inventory availability tend to achieve stronger sales outcomes.

Product availability is important for converting customer demand into revenue.

---

## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

Leadership should prioritize:

* Increasing customer traffic
* Improving inventory availability
* Optimizing marketing investments
* Understanding successful practices in South and West regions

These factors demonstrated meaningful relationships with sales performance.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

The North region indicator showed weaker statistical evidence.

Variables with limited statistical significance should not be treated as major business drivers without additional analysis.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

1. Invest in initiatives that increase store traffic.
2. Monitor marketing return on investment.
3. Reduce stock-out risk through inventory planning.
4. Investigate operational practices used by high-performing stores.
5. Conduct additional analysis on stores with unusually large residuals.

---

## Risks and Limitations

Several factors influencing sales are not included in the dataset, including:

* Local market conditions
* Competitor actions
* Store management quality
* Promotional campaigns
* Economic conditions

As a result, the model should support decisions rather than replace business judgment.

---

## Association vs Causation

Regression analysis measures statistical association between variables.

A significant coefficient does not automatically prove that one variable directly causes changes in sales.

For example, higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales, but other factors could also influence both variables.

Therefore, management should treat regression results as evidence of relationships rather than proof of causality.

---

## Conclusion

The final multiple regression model explains approximately 81.1% of variation in monthly sales.

The strongest evidence supports focusing on:

* Footfall
* Marketing Spend
* Inventory Availability

These areas are likely to provide the greatest opportunity for improving store sales performance.
