# Residual Analysis

## Method

Predicted sales values were calculated using the selected multiple regression model.

Residuals were calculated using:

Residual = Actual Sales − Predicted Sales

Positive residuals indicate stores that performed better than predicted.

Negative residuals indicate stores that performed worse than predicted.

---

## Largest Positive Residuals

The five largest positive residual records represent stores where actual sales substantially exceeded model predictions.

Possible explanations include:

* Exceptional local promotions
* Strong store management
* Local events increasing customer demand
* Customer loyalty effects not captured in the model
* Competitive advantages not included in the dataset

These stores may provide useful operational insights for management.

---

## Largest Negative Residuals

The five largest negative residual records represent stores where actual sales were significantly below model predictions.

Possible explanations include:

* Operational inefficiencies
* Temporary stock shortages
* Local competitive pressure
* Staffing challenges
* Economic conditions not captured by the model

These stores may require additional investigation.

---

## Business Interpretation

The residual analysis indicates that while the model explains a large proportion of sales variation, additional factors influence performance.

Stores with large positive residuals may contain best practices worth replicating.

Stores with large negative residuals may require corrective action or further analysis.

---

## Model Performance Assessment

The model does not appear to consistently over-predict or under-predict all stores.

However, some individual stores show substantial deviations from expected performance, indicating the presence of additional business factors not included in the regression model.

Overall, the residual distribution supports the use of the selected model as a decision-support tool while recognizing its limitations.
